    Let $f(x)$ be a $\mathbb{R}$. Then $f(x)$ is a function of $x$ if for each $x$ in the domain of $f(x)$, there is exactly one value of $f(x)$.
    
    \(\text{Let, \space}f(x)=9+5\text{\space where \space}9\neq 0\)
    
\begin{equation} 
    \begin{aligned}
        f(x) &= 9+5 & \text{Simplify.} \\
        &= 14 & \text{Add.} \\
    \end{aligned}
\end{equation}
